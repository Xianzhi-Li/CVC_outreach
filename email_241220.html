2025 New Years greeting:
CVC facing Challenges and Opportunities


During the early years we witnessed significant growth spurts as Dhamma spread worldwide. This spread was not a straight line upwards and Goenkaji would often comment on his and Sayagyi's experience - that whenever Dhamma is on the cusp of making a significant advance, opposing forces would rise as a challenge. Over the decades we have seen this occur repeatedly and today is no exception.


In nearly every corner of the world the pace of the spread of Dhamma is quickening. There is only one reason to account for this: the rising volition of students to share the benefits of Dhamma practice with others. It does not take much reflection for each of us to realize how our lives took a significant turn for the better when we first encountered Vipassana. And with the practice we feel the ongoing rising compassion and mettā that spurs the volition to share Dhamma further.


Established centers now play their role in trying to expand and improve the conditions for course students. Both the geographic spread and the personal development of the depth of Dhamma practice are important - each complements the other.


CVC is now poised to increase its contribution to the spread of Dhamma as it improves and attracts students locally and globally. But at present, challenges are rising: cost over-runs for two major projects here have brought the center's finances to a point where CVC will run out of development funds by Spring 2025. Though Center course operations are not imperiled current and future projects will be stalled pending additional donations.


When completed in Spring 2025 the new Women's Servers Quarters will house 12 servers in individual rooms each with an ensuite bath similar to the quad rooms for students. This will greatly improve the conditions for women servers while at the same time allow us to expand the women's dining room. Both the present women servers’ rooms and the women's dining rooms have been crying out for improvement for some time.


Repairs to the Pagoda cell complex due to years of water damage are nearing completion though the resurfacing of the terrace will likely be delayed due to shortage of funds.


View Project Flyers

At the same time as these immediate challenges rise CVC has embarked on a significant development vision to complete the center within 5 years. This moon-shot project aims 1) to increase the number of private student rooms with ensuite baths to 100 with twenty additional rooms to be able to serve up to 120 students. And 2) To build a new dedicated kitchen and women's dining room. This will bring CVC near to full build out and closer to Goenkaji's definition of an ideal meditation center where each student has the needed privacy for serious meditation and residence. Upon completion, both the new students on regular 10-day courses as well as the old students on longer more serious courses will surely benefit. With these near ideal conditions, CVC will be a beacon for new centers to emulate thus propelling the wheel of Dhamma to continue to turn benefiting more and more people well into the future.


To sum up:

CVC is embarking on a significant leap forward to better serve students and servers attending courses at the center. The goal of Improving current conditions to achieve near ideal ones for longer and deeper meditation courses is within reach. May this information of this unique dana opportunity fall on receptive ears. May all beings be happy.
